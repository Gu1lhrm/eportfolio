Course Reflections

At the beginning of the course, my knowledge of writing, research, and communication was more geared towards the academic or informal world (friends and family) rather than user-centered communication. Because of this, one of my biggest challenges was developing the "Fast Fix" guide, where I had to present technical information to users without a technical background. To create a page ideal for the experience of a typical iFixit user, I had to change my use of words like "detach" or "remove" to more detailed, step-by-step descriptions accompanied by detailed images of the current step. This taught me that effective writing is not just about conveying information about a subject, but about making sure people from different backgrounds understand what I'm trying to communicate. Furthermore, I learned to work with peer feedback more easily, allowing me to focus on and improve aspects of my projects that are still unclear to users and enhancing my ability to provide feedback and work in a group. This knowledge will help me work on creating internal manuals and projects that require direct communication with the client.
